Data travels in packet. Each packet  can carry a maximum of 1,500 bytes.
Around the packets, there is a wrapper with a header and a footer and
that contains information that tells computers what type of data is in
the packet.

An IP address is what is assigned to a computer/server allowing it to be
identified on a network. A DSN address converts alphabetic references into
a server's IP address.

Cookies are most commonly used to track website activity. They are messages
that web servers pass to your web browser when you visit internet sites.
